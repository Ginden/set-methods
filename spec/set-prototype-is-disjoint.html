When the `isDisjoint` method is called, the following steps are taken:

1. Let _set_ be the *this* value. 1. If Type(_set_) is not Object, throw a *TypeError* exception. 1. If _set_ does not have a [[SetData]] internal slot, throw a *TypeError* exception. 1. Let _entries_ be the List that is _set_.[[SetData]]. 1. If Type(_iterable_) is not Object, throw a *TypeError* exception. 1. If _iterable_ does not have [[SetData]] internal slot, 1. Let _otherSet_ be ? Construct(%Set%, _iterable_). 1. Else, let _otherSet_ be _iterable_. 1. Let _hasCheck_ be ? Get(_otherSet_, "has"). 1. If IsCallable(_hasCheck_) is *false*, throw a *TypeError* exception. 1. For each _e_ that is an element of _entries_, do 1. Let _has_ be ? Call(_hasCheck_, _otherSet_, « e ») 1. If _has_ is *true*, return *false*. 1. return *true*.

The `length` property of the `isDisjoint` method is *1*.
